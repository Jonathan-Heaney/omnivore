Submission Guidelines

Have questions about what kind of art you can/should submit? Here are some general guidelines.

We take an expansive view of “the arts.”

We’re thinking broadly — visual arts, performing arts, literary arts, and more.

Examples that fit well:

  • Architecture
  • Dance
  • Fiction
  • Film
  • Music
  • Painting
  • Photography
  • Poetry
  • Sculpture
  • Television
  • Theatre

Examples that are usually less suitable:

  • News/opinion articles
  • Most non-fiction books
  • Memes
  • Recipes
  • Sports highlights
Lean toward pieces slightly off the beaten path.

It’s great to love Abbey Road, Game of Thrones, or Harry Potter — but those works don’t need help spreading, and many recipients will already know them. Aim for something wonderful that a fair number of people might not know yet.

That said, if you have a fascinating personal story about a well-known piece, share it! Seeing familiar work through your unique lens can be powerful.

Prefer pieces people can easily experience online.

If you’re torn between a novel that costs money/time and a song that’s freely available online, choose the song. The more accessible the piece, the better the sharing experience.

But if you really want people to know about that novel — go for it.

Be thoughtful about what you write.

No need for a five-paragraph essay, but everyone benefits when you share something personal and meaningful — why it matters to you, how you found it, what to notice, or the story behind your pick.
